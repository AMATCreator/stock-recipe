Homemade stock recipe for borsch

This stock takes some time to make, but the result is a very delicious, rich broth to usse as a base for your borsch.

Ingredients

  • Beef bones, with meat (or shank of beef) if you will cook the soup right after
  • 1 Onion
  • 4 Garlic
  • 1 Carrot
  • 3 Celery stems
  • 1 leek (green part)
  • 2 Bay leafs
  • 7l Water
  • dried herbs:1tbsp whole Peppercorns, 1tbsp coriander seeds, 2tbsp marjoram, 2tbsp chervil (you can put the herbs in 1 or 2 teabags for convenience)

Instructions

  1. Put a large pot of water (approximately 7l) to a boil, lightly salted.
  2. Wash the meat and the bones and add them to the boiling water. Let it softly boil for about 4 hours. Remove the floating guck and foam regularly.
  3. Take out the meat and put aside to cool. Sift the stock through a cheese cloth into another pot, repeat this process 2 or three times untill the stock is clear.
  4. Put the stock again to boil and add the rest of the ingredients and let the whole simmer for 2 or 3 more hours. You will notice that the liquid is reduced, a reduction of 1/3 is good, 1/2 is also ok, if it has reduced more, you can add water later. Don't forget to skim the surface regularly to keep the stock clear.
  5. Take out the vergetables and herbs, then sift the stock through a cheese cloth in another pot. Repeat the process 2 or 3 times until the stock is as clear as possible.
  6. Now you stock is ready to use. You can directly use the stock to start cooking your delicious borsch or you can freeze the stock in batches to use later.
