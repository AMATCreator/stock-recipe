Grandma's Delicious Borsch

This is one of those recipes that just warms your soul and is perfect for a cold winters day. This delicious recipe has passed on from my grandma to my mother and then to me. The original recipe includes making your own stock. It does take some time to make, but the result definitely makes it worth the effort and time.Check out the steps to make your own stock here .

Ingredients

  • 5l of stock
  • 500gr Beef soup meat or schank of beef (shredded)
  • about 15 whole black pepper corns
  • 1 big onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves of garlic (chopped)
  • 1/2 white cabbage (sliced thinly)
  • 2-3 ripe tomatoes (cut in cubes)
  • 1 big carrot or 2 medium sized ones (cut to bitesize pieces)
  • 5 middlesized potatoes (cut in bitesized cubes)
  • 1 can of tomatoe paste
  • 3 bay leafs
  • 4 red beets (cut in thin slices)
  • 1 lemon
  • salt and pepper
  • fresh herbs for serving (basil, dille, coriander, parsley, spring onion)
  • sour cream or mayo for serving

Instructions

  1. Put a pot of water to a boil, salt the water lightly.
  2. Wash the beef meat and put it in the boiling water (water should cover the meat completely and then some) together with the whole pepper corns, boil until the meat is soft.
  3. When the meat is soft, take it out of the water, let it cool a bit and then shred it to bite size pieces, toss the water away.
  4. Bring the stock to a boil in a big pot and add the shredded beef.
  5. Add the sliced white cabbage and let it boil softly for 10 min.
  6. Add the onion to the broth together with the garlic, carrots, tomatoes, tomato paste, potatoes and the bay leafs. Let the broth boil for 10 min.
  7. Meanwhile peel and cut the red beets into thin slices, put them in a bowl and drip some lemon juice over them to keep a fresh color. Then add them to the broth.
  8. Let the whole simmer on low heat for about 20min or until the beets and potatoes are soft to eat.
  9. Turn of the heat and add salt and pepper to your liking.
  10. Serve the soup with a big hand of thinly sliced fresh herb mix and some sour cream or mayo served separately.
  11. Enjoy!

Tip: For a better result, heat up the ingredients like onion, garlic, carrot, tomatoes, tomato paste and red beet in a frying pan before adding them to the soup, this will prevent your soup from cooling down every time you a dd new ingredient.

Tip: Make sure you skeem the foam of the surface regularly to ensure your broth will be as clear as possible. This also removes the guck and gelatine substance that float up from your meat that would otherwise cloud your broth and influence the taste.
